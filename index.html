My Story

With a Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering, I am currently a graduate student at the University of Alberta, where I am pursuing a Master of Science in Electrical Engineering, and I have a research assistantship with the Computer Science department. I have been working on robotic control and automation for more than 6 years now. I enjoy standing at the intersection of these three major fields and working with people of different mindsets. In my current role as a Graduate Research Fellow, my work involves developing real-time controllers for robotic arms, diagnosing and rectifying hardware issues, and securing research grants by presenting lab demos to influential stakeholders. My technical toolkit includes Python, C++, and ROS, and I've successfully applied machine learning techniques to solve complex robotic challenges. I'm passionate about the synergy between engineering and AI, and I aspire to bridge that gap to drive innovation. My interdisciplinary background, technical expertise, and collaborative spirit make me a versatile and valuable contributor to various projects and teams.
