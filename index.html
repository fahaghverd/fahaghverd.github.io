Transparency-Optimized Position-Position Bilateral Teleoperation

   

My research here focused on improving position-based bilateral teleoperation, trying to make haptic-guided teleoperation more accessible outside of research platforms. This research explores a position-position (P-P) architecture, which infers interaction forces through position tracking. It proposes a transparency-optimized P-P control scheme incorporating dynamic compensation, validated through theoretical and experimental analysis on Barrett WAM robotic arms. Results indicate significant improvements in position tracking errors (up to 94%), free motion impedance reduction (80-94%), while keeping force tracking error below 5% and preserving simplicity. The study demonstrates P-P architecture's potential to achieve transparency levels comparable to more complex systems, providing insights for practical teleoperation applications.

Deep Probabilistic Movement Primitives

   

In our project, we introduced a deep movement primitive architecture that encodes all the operations found in linear alternatives, with the added benefit of a Bayesian context aggregator for improved context conditioning and blending. To evaluate our model, we conducted performance comparisons with existing baselines in the literature, using two commonly used robotic arms, the WAM and KINOVA arms.

Simulating Robot-Assisted Surgery; Robot to Camera Coordinate Registration

       

This project focuses on the critical integration of medical imaging and robotic arm control in robot-assisted Minimally Invasive Surgery (MIS). Within this semi-autonomous system, surgeons specify surgical procedures with precise needle insertion coordinates. The workflow includes calibration to align the imaging and robotic systems, translating surgeon commands to the robot's coordinates, and executing precise needle positioning on a disassembled human model mannequin. We use the WAM arm for simulation in MoveIt and real-world implementation.

Uncalibrated Visual Servoing (UVS)

   

In our project, we addressed Uncalibrated Visual Servoing (UVS) with a focus on both 2D and 3D tracking. For 2D tracking, we used the Lucas-Kanade tracker, and for 3D tracking, we employed MTF-based trackers. We also developed controllers for position and velocity to ensure precise robotic control. To optimize robot motion planning, we utilized curved splines with Bezier curves for smooth and accurate trajectory planning.

Learning Inverse Dynamic Model for 7-DoF WAM arm

     

Model-based control systems are among most efficient and reliable robotic control systems. However, extracting manipulator’s dynamic modelling is challenging. Here, a one-layer MLP neural network is used to model the lumped function H, which allows for estimation of joint torques based on current joint states. To create a dataset for learning inverse dynamics, I used Python scripts with ROS to generate normally distributed trajectories with position and velocity as inputs and torque as the output. This dataset was then used to train the MLP neural network for estimating joint torque based on the robot's configuration, implementing inverse dynamics.
